The Manuscripts of C. S. Peirce

Charles Sanders Peirce (1839–1914) — logician, scientist, the founder of pragmatism — left some hundred thousand pages unpublished at his death. These are some of his manuscripts at Harvard's Houghton Library, hosted on the Internet Archive in the public domain, shown here with machine-assisted draft transcriptions still under correction. The hand is his, and so are the wax seal above and the signature below.
