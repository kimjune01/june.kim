Bibliography

The transcriptions in this viewer are machine-assisted drafts, still under correction. For the authoritative scholarly editions and the reference apparatus that situates each manuscript, the works below are where to go. Where a manuscript has a critical edition of its own, it is listed under Editions of individual manuscripts.

Standard editions & finding aids

Richard S. Robin, Annotated Catalogue of the Papers of Charles S. Peirce
University of Massachusetts Press, 1967 (with a 1971 supplement).
The source of the R and L numbers used throughout this viewer. Every item here is keyed to its Robin number.
Charles Hartshorne, Paul Weiss & Arthur W. Burks (eds.), Collected Papers of Charles Sanders Peirce
8 vols. Harvard University Press, 1931–1958. Cited as CP volume.paragraph.
Peirce Edition Project (eds.), Writings of Charles S. Peirce: A Chronological Edition
Indiana University Press, 1982– (in progress). The modern critical edition; cited as W.
Nathan Houser & Christian Kloesel (eds.), The Essential Peirce
2 vols. Indiana University Press, 1992 & 1998. Cited as EP.
Carolyn Eisele (ed.), The New Elements of Mathematics by Charles S. Peirce
4 vols. in 5. Mouton, 1976. Cited as NEM.
Commens — Digital Companion to C. S. Peirce
Bibliography, dictionary of Peirce's terms, and manuscript catalogue entries.
Arisbe: The Peirce Gateway
Joseph Ransdell's long-running archive of transcriptions, reconstructions, and secondary work.
Harvard Houghton Library — Charles S. Peirce Papers (MS Am 1632)
The holding library. The page images in this viewer are these manuscripts, hosted on the Internet Archive in the public domain.
The detailed HOLLIS for Archival Discovery finding aid gives the full arrangement of the ~100,000 pages and links to digitized documents.

Editions of individual manuscripts

R L75The Carnegie Institution application (1902)
Peirce's 1902 application to the Carnegie Institution for support to write his logic — the memoir "Logic, Regarded as Semeiotic" — together with the surrounding correspondence.
Joseph Ransdell, MS L75: Logic, Regarded as Semeiotic (The Carnegie Application of 1902), Version 1 — a topical reconstruction that redistributes the six draft versions across Peirce's 36 proposed memoirs.
Carolyn Eisele (ed.), "Parts of Carnegie Application (L 75)," in The New Elements of Mathematics, vol. 4, pp. 13–73.
Commens catalogue entry.
Both editions treat the application memoir. The recommendation letters and administrative correspondence in the folder — including William James's letter urging Peirce's case as "one for favorable treatment" — are transcribed here as searchable text.

Other bibliographies

Kenneth Laine Ketner et al., A Comprehensive Bibliography of the Published Works of Charles Sanders Peirce
2nd ed., revised. Philosophy Documentation Center, 1986.
The counterpart to Robin: where the Robin catalogue covers the manuscripts, Ketner covers everything Peirce published, with a bibliography of secondary studies through 1984.
Charles Sanders Peirce bibliography (Wikipedia)
A free, continually updated list of Peirce's primary works and the major secondary literature — the most convenient starting point online.

Research centers & societies

The Charles S. Peirce Society
United States. Founded 1946 — the oldest Peirce organization; publisher of Transactions of the Charles S. Peirce Society.
Peirce Edition Project
Indiana University Indianapolis. The critical-edition center behind the Writings (W), holding an extensive collection of primary and secondary materials.
Grupo de Estudios Peirceanos
Universidad de Navarra, Spain. Peirce studies for the Spanish-speaking world, with a large library of texts in Spanish translation.
Centro de Estudos de Pragmatismo
PUC–São Paulo, Brazil. The Brazilian center for pragmatism; publisher of the journal Cognitio and host of the International Meetings on Pragmatism.
Helsinki Peirce Research Centre & the Nordic Pragmatism Network
University of Helsinki, Finland. The home of Peirce studies in the Nordic countries; its members maintain the Commens portal listed above.

Reference & orientation

Charles Sanders Peirce — Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy
A book-length scholarly overview of Peirce's logic, semiotic, pragmatism, and metaphysics — the best single place to begin.
Charles Sanders Peirce — Wikipedia
General biography and orientation, with links onward to the primary and secondary literature.
