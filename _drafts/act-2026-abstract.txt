A Lean-Verified Crosswalk Between Stochastic Semantics, Contracts, and Hoare Logic

June Kim

We machine-check in Lean 4 (2,800 lines, zero sorry, standard library only) that behavioral contracts on stochastic morphisms and Hoare triples over FinStoch are the same structure, proved from two independent angles -- one functional, one imperative -- whose equivalence is compiled, not just described.

The development models an information-processing pipeline as a Kleisli composition of stochastic kernels. Each stage carries a contract: a predicate on outputs preserved under monadic composition. A separate Hoare-logic layer builds Hoare triples and weakest preconditions from scratch, then a bridge theorem shows that ContractPreserving f Q is equivalent to {T} f {Q} -- a Hoare triple with trivial precondition. Both proof paths reach the same pipeline correctness result; Lean checks their agreement. A nontriviality counterexample rules out vacuous readings of the specification.

This dual proof assembles a precise crosswalk across communities that have independently formalized overlapping structures under different names:

- Markov categories <-> Kleisli(D). The ambient category is Kleisli(D) = FinStoch, the paradigmatic Markov category (Fritz 2020). The Lean Support typeclass is the possibilistic shadow of Fritz, Perrone, and Rezagholi (2021): support_bind is the monad multiplication for supp : V => H.

- Imperative categories <-> contract composition. Bonchi, Di Lavore, Roman, and Staton (2025) prove that Stoch is a posetal imperative category (Corollary 76) and derive Hoare rules as theorems (Theorem 79). The Lean bridge theorem is an independent verification of this identification: behavioral contracts on stochastic morphisms are Hoare triples.

- Graded program logics <-> handshake conditions. The contract predicate is close to a graded Hoare postcondition (Gaboardi et al. 2021); the handshake condition between adjacent stages resembles the restricted pointwise order in graded-monadic program logics (Kura et al. 2026). These correspondences are plausible rather than exact -- the ambient categories differ.

- Categorical cybernetics <-> compositional predicates. Selection relations on parametrised optics (Capucci et al. 2021) and compositional equilibrium predicates (Ghani, Hedges et al. 2018) play the same structural role as contracts: behavioral predicates preserved under composition. The ambient structures (optics, games) differ from Kleisli.

The crosswalk surfaces open problems visible only from the intersection: (1) the free N-semimodule monad (Kidney & Wu 2021) composed with the support monad morphism would track support cardinality through stochastic composition, connecting Leinster's magnitude (2021) to Fritz's possibilistic shadow -- this composite has not been constructed; (2) determinantal point processes have no categorical semantics; (3) Fritz's categorical entropy has no accompanying achievability or rate-distortion results inside Markov categories.

Keywords: Markov categories, Hoare logic, Lean 4, possibilistic shadow, graded monads, imperative categories, weakest precondition, compositional contracts

The talk is aimed at researchers who work in one of these formalisms but not the others.

The Lean development is at github.com/kimjune01/natural-framework. A browsable Rosetta stone with the full crosswalk tables is at june.kim/framework-lexicon.
