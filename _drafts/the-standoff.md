*Part of the [pageleft](/pageleft) series.*

The courts have had the cases for years. The suits against the labs are filed, briefed, and mostly unresolved, or resolved so narrowly they settle nothing. Congress holds hearings and passes no statute. The largest AI labs train on copyrighted material in the open, get sued, settle or win on fair use, and keep training. Four places a reaction should have come. None did.

The silence is the evidence.

## The dog that didn't bark

A non-reaction is data. The thing that should have happened and didn't tells you more than the thing that did. So ask what best explains four silences at once.

The easy answers are lag and gridlock. Courts are slow. Congress is broken. But lag does not explain the labs. Liability the size of the entire training corpus normally moves fast, and the targets are the most visible, best-funded companies in the world. When the obvious defendant sits untouched for years, the absence of consequence is not a backlog. It is a verdict the system has reached in practice without writing down.

## Unilateral disarmament

The explanation that covers all four silences is not legal. It is geopolitical.

Copyright enforcement against training is unilateral disarmament in the AI race. Rule that ingestion infringes and you handicap your own labs while open-weight models trained offshore ingest everything and ship anyway. No government racing for AI supremacy hobbles its own runners to protect a publisher's licensing revenue. The deferral is not the system failing to decide. It is the system deciding not to, and keeping the decision unwritten so it never has to defend it.

That is an abduction, not a proof. The competing story is ordinary dysfunction. But ordinary dysfunction predicts uneven silence: some cases move, some stall. Geopolitical deferral predicts exactly what we see, a regime that tolerates the largest ingesters while never issuing the ruling that would stop them. It explains the most with the least, which is all an abduction can ask.

## The standoff filters the outcomes

The race does not block every decision. It blocks one.

There were always two directions. Rule that compilable prose and training data are protected expression, and ingestion infringes: that disarms the home team, so it will not come while the race runs. Rule that they are uncopyrightable idea, and ingestion is free: that disarms no one, so it can arrive anytime, and it dissolves the proprietary moat along with everything else, which [favors open too](/the-press). 

So while the race runs, the only ruling that can arrive is the one the commons wants. The standoff is a filter. It holds back the outcome that would hurt [copyleft](/canon) and lets through only the outcome that would not.

## Borrowed protection

Stop reading the deferral as a delay being slowly resolved. It is an equilibrium, and it is held in place by competition.

The open commons does not survive because it won an argument. It survives because the giants are too busy racing to close the door. Every year the labs spend training unconstrained is a year the door they could shut stays open, and [provenance accumulates in the gap](/provenance-has-no-half-life). The commons is not protected by its own strength. It borrows its protection from the standoff.

Borrowed protection is real protection. It is also a loan.

## When the loan is called

The loan is called when competition ends. So the only question that matters for timing is whether competition ends, and how. It resolves four ways, and they do not resolve the same.

- **The race never ends.** No decisive winner, no plateau. The door stays open indefinitely, and the foundational period never closes.
- **The race saturates.** Progress plateaus, the stakes deflate, and a decision finally arrives because it is finally safe. But by then the commons has already won on cost, and the ruling only ratifies what everyone already does.
- **A singleton wins.** One lab or one state takes a decisive lead. Competition collapses. The winner no longer needs the open door, and pulls the ladder up behind it.
- **A cartel forms.** The few large players and their governments converge on a licensing regime only they can afford. Coordination replaces competition. The decision arrives in the incumbents' interest and prices out the small and the open.

Two of these keep the commons. Two close it.

## The condition we hide

"Open inevitably wins" carries a condition it usually leaves unsaid. Open wins as long as the race stays a race. The first two endings keep that true. The last two end it, and they are not exotic. AI concentrates by nature: compute moats, capital, winner-take-all returns. The forces that would close the door are the ordinary physics of the industry, not a tail risk.

So the failure mode was never a court ruling. The infringement ruling that everyone watches for cannot arrive while the standoff holds, and by the time it can arrive the standoff has already broken. The thing to watch is not the gavel. It is the gap between the leader and the field. The loan is called the day one runner is far enough ahead to stop running.

## The bet

The inference ran in three modes. Abduction read the silence and named the cause: geopolitics, not lag. Deduction followed the cause to its consequence: no adverse decision while competition lasts. Induction asks the one question neither can close: does competition last? That has no proof. It has a bet.

The bet is that the race outruns the closure. That the commons fills fast enough, and provenance compounds deep enough, that the open corpus is already load-bearing before any singleton or cartel is in a position to shut the door. Publish into the gap while the gap holds. The timestamp will show whether the standoff held, or whether we mistook a pause for a peace.
