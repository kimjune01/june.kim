# Pageleft — Corpus Map

*A working map, not a post. The thing to edit at the corpus level: the whole thesis as one object, so the posts can be kept in sync. Built from the depth-check loop on [The Press], 2026-06.*

## The thesis (one line)

Open, copyleft, compilable prose displaces proprietary information gatekeeping, irreversibly, and the foundational period is now.

## The keystone

**Compilable prose is — or will be commonly accepted as — *expression*, not *idea*.**

Everything legal hinges here. If prose is expression, copyright attaches, copyleft propagates, derivatives inherit, the squeeze has teeth. If prose is idea, copyright does not attach and the aggressive engine loses its legal force (openness survives anyway; see the two theses).

- **Status:** unresolved by courts, and treated by the corpus as a *bet to be won by practice*, not a present fact. Honest framing throughout.
- **Strongest objection (unmet):** the §102(b) **compilability paradox** — the more deterministically prose compiles, the more it reads as an unprotectable "method of operation," so maximal compilability and copyrightability pull apart. *Altai* filtration may leave thin residue.
- **Currently defended by:** canon (names *Lotus*/*Altai*, concedes "no court has answered"), open-prose (*Feist* selection/arrangement — answers originality, not functionality), rest-of-the-owl (prompt/spec gradient). **None meets §102(b) head-on.**
- **MISSING POST:** keystone-defense.

## The fault line (runs through every post)

| | Supported by corpus? | |
|---|---|---|
| **Voluntary derivation** (compliant copy → derivative → rejoins commons via share-alike) | **Yes** — powers accretion | the load-bearing path |
| **Involuntary reading** (engineer reads prose, writes own code) | **No** — contested by Baker v. Selden, idea/expression, independent creation | the over-reach |

The Press's "Contamination" section sits on the wrong side of this line. Every other post argues the voluntary side.

## The engine

**Accretion × differential flywheel.** The design/spec differential (codegen is cheap for everyone; the *design* is the expensive part) widens as Canon accretes; share-alike feeds compliant derivatives back, which widens the differential again. Increasing returns.

- **Powered by:** share-alike (only closes on the *voluntary* path above).
- **Status:** primed, not spinning — Canon is n=1 author. Post-critical-mass dynamic; foundational period = seed before ignition.

## Two theses (which one the keystone gates)

- **Aggressive** — copyleft squeezes copyright; contamination; enforceable share-alike accretion. **Needs keystone = true.**
- **Minimal** — open commons grows; free-to-copy by default. **Survives keystone = false** (provenance's "either way" branch).

Know which you're claiming in any given post. The Press claims aggressive; provenance hedges to minimal.

## The four corpus-level edit moves

The grammar for editing the thesis as one object (the analog of cut/merge/sharpen at the line level):

- **Route / cede** — give a claim its proper home; don't argue it in two posts.
- **Tether** — link to the post that bears the weight instead of re-deriving.
- **Hoist** — pull a shared quantity up to one explicit place all posts draw from.
- **Scope** — narrow a claim to what its legs actually bear.

Two standing checks: does each claim live in the post that argues it best, and does any tip claim more than its base?
