# Computational Theology — Fan-out

## Hypothesis

Surviving theological doctrines must align with the Natural Framework's six roles (Perceive → Cache → Filter → Attend → Transmit → Consolidate) because memetic darwinian selection over centuries would have filtered out cognitively incorrect structures. Theology as the best-recorded memetic substrate. If the Natural Framework is right, the winners of creedal debates must encode:

- **Type enforcement** — what counts as valid (valid prayer, valid sacrament, valid prophecy)
- **Preconditions** — states required before the operation "works"
- **Postconditions** — what is guaranteed if the operation succeeds

And the big claim: the traditions that survived the longest should show the cleanest computational structure, because the messy ones lost debates.

## Caveats going in

Survival ≠ truth. State backing, coercion, literacy infrastructure, and Constantinople all weighted the fitness landscape. Any subagent that finds alignment must also account for how much of the survival was memetic-fitness vs. political-fitness. And the Natural Framework is the user's own model — confirmation bias is a standing threat.

## Angles

- **H1** — Sacramental validity (matter, form, minister, intent) as literal type enforcement
- **H2** — Ritual purity codes across traditions as actor/material preconditions
- **H3** — Contemplative methods as explicit cognitive pipelines mapped to the six roles
- **H4** — Creedal debates as the memetic selection arena — what invariants survived, what got filtered
- **H5** — Confession/teshuvah/metanoia as the consolidation protocol (backward pass with parameter updates)

### H1: Sacramental validity (opus, partial)

**Verdict:** Matter/form/minister/intent is a genuine precondition check with postcondition guarantees, at a level a type-checker would recognize. But its survival is confounded with imperial and curial enforcement, so the fitness-from-correctness leg is not established here.

**Claims (with provenance):**
- **Trent, Session 7 (1547).** Canon 11 (general): minister must have "the intention at least of doing what the Church does," under anathema. On Baptism, canon 2 requires "true and natural water" as matter; canon 4 recognizes baptism by heretics *iff* trinitarian formula and right intent. Four named fields, each with an explicit invalidation clause. (Source: papalencyclicals.net transcription; no critical Latin edition consulted.)
- **1983 CIC can. 849:** baptism "is validly conferred only by a washing in real water with the proper form of words." One-sentence validity predicate, two typed arguments.
- **CDF Responsum ad dubium (6 Aug 2020, Francis approving):** "*We* baptize you…" is null; recipients must be rebaptized *in forma absoluta*. Minister acts *in persona Christi*, not as delegate of the assembly. A one-pronoun substitution retroactively invalidates. Cleanest modern Filter-fire. (Source: vatican.va; USCCB newsletter July/Aug 2020.)
- **Leo XIII, *Apostolicae Curae* (1896):** Anglican orders "absolutely null and utterly void" for "defect of form and intention" — the Edwardine Ordinal dropped sacrificing-priesthood language. Accept the verdict or not, the *argument* is a type-signature mismatch.
- **Aquinas, *ST* III q.60 and q.64:** matter + form (*res et verba*) as the constitutive pair; minister and intent as further requirements. (Question numbers verified via newadvent.org; articles not read in full.)

**Mapping to the six roles:**
- Matter/form/intent are **Filter** preconditions — 2020 "we baptize" is literally a Filter fire.
- Minister is the **Transmit** boundary. *In persona Christi* is why solo first-person is required: Transmit is one-sender by contract.
- "Indelible character" (baptism, confirmation, orders) is a **Consolidate** write: one-shot, idempotent. Matches store→parameter. *Ex opere operato* is the claim the write fires whenever Filter passes.
- **Strain:** transubstantiation changes *the matter itself*, not an agent's parameters. Six-role frame has no clean cell for "change to the Cache object."

**Failure modes (adversarial):**
- **Donatism.** Donatists held minister-holiness as a precondition; Augustine and the imperial church ruled otherwise. *Ex opere operato* is cleaner as a type *and* what the post-Diocletian church needed to function. Correctness and convenience point the same way — signals confounded.
- **Intent is leaky.** "Doing what the Church does" is unobservable. A real type-checker rejects unobservable preconditions; sacramental theology accepts them and tolerates doubt cases.
- **Anglican orders dispute.** *Saepius Officio* (1897) argued Leo XIII misread the Edwardine form. Two traditions with the same precondition logic reach opposite verdicts on a 450-year-old rubric. Clean type systems do not behave this way.
- **Orthodox oikonomia.** Clergy from Rome/Canterbury sometimes received without reordination, by pastoral economy. A type system with a universal override has runtime casts.

**Memetic-fitness honest read:** Trent crystallized the schema under counter-Reformation pressure; the Donatist outcome was settled by imperial edict (405) before theology closed. Both political. Against that: the schema still adjudicates live cases in 2020 — schemas backed by empire usually mutate when the empire does, this one hasn't. Weak evidence the structure carries its own weight. Empire + literacy monopoly + curial bureaucracy are sufficient to explain survival; correctness and power are confounded.

**Dead if applicable:** Partial alive. Type-enforcement analogy is real, six-role mapping non-trivial; memetic-fitness leg not established.

**Open questions:**
- Any validity ruling that went *against* state/curial interest and won? Evidence of truth-selection over power-selection.
- Did Orthodox sacramental theology converge on matter/form/intent independently, or is the explicit schema a Latin artifact?
- Does "indelible character" (one-shot Consolidate) recur elsewhere — chrismation, upanayana, Shingon abhisheka — or is it Latin-specific?

### H4: Creedal selection arena (opus, partial)

**Verdict:** Invariant-preservation reading works for 2-3 cases, but the political-fitness counter-case is strong enough that "darwinian selection" cannot be load-bearing without heavy qualification. Survival tracks empire more cleanly than cognitive correctness. The framework is compatible with the data but not selected by it.

**Four debates across two traditions:**

1. **Arianism vs. Nicea (325, reaffirmed Constantinople 381).** Arius: the Son was created, "there was when he was not," first and highest of creatures. Nicea: *homoousios* — same substance as the Father. Invariant preserved: the type signature of "worthy of latreutic worship" must be closed under the incarnation. If Christ is worshipped and Christ is created, worship of a creature follows — idolatry. Athanasius makes this the explicit argument in *Contra Arianos* (soteriological: only God can deify). Primary documents: Nicene Creed, Athanasius *Contra Arianos*, *Tome* of 381. Lewis Ayres (*Nicaea and Its Legacy*, 2004) frames this as a grammar of divinity stabilizing over fifty years, not a one-shot victory.

2. **Chalcedon vs. Monophysitism (451).** Eutyches: one nature after the union. Chalcedon: two natures, one person, "without confusion, without change, without division, without separation." Invariant preserved: a type union where neither component erases the other — Christ must be fully human (to redeem humanity) and fully divine (to have standing to redeem). Primary: Chalcedonian Definition. Jaroslav Pelikan (*The Christian Tradition* vol. 1) reads the four adverbs as negative fencing: the council refused to specify the mechanism, only the invariants. Andrew Louth (*Introducing Eastern Orthodox Theology*) makes the same point. Genuinely a "type signature without implementation" move.

3. **Mu'tazila vs. Ash'ari on the created Qur'an (9th c.).** Mu'tazila: the Qur'an is created; God's unity (tawhid) forbids co-eternal attributes. Ash'ari: the Qur'an is uncreated speech of God; attributes subsist in the essence without being other than it. Invariant preserved: distinction between divine speech and creation without collapsing tawhid. Primary: al-Ash'ari's *Ibāna* and *Maqalat al-Islamiyyin*. But the *mihna* (inquisition, 833-848 under al-Ma'mun and successors) enforced Mu'tazili doctrine by state power first; Ash'arism won only after state support shifted under al-Mutawakkil — a cleanly political inversion.

4. **Talmudic Hillel/Shammai disputes.** The Talmud records Shammai's school losing almost all disputes with Hillel's. Bavli Eruvin 13b: a bat kol declares "these and these are words of the living God, but the halakha follows Beit Hillel" — the meta-principle is that both schools encode truth but practical rulings follow the humbler one. Invariant: pluralism in sources, univocality in practice. A different shape from creedal selection — disagreement built into the substrate rather than filtered out.

**Political-fitness counter-case (the serious objection):** Of the seven councils recognized as ecumenical, at least five were convoked by emperors (Constantine, Theodosius I, Theodosius II, Marcian, Constantine IV, Irene). Chalcedon fractured the Church into Chalcedonian / Oriental Orthodox / Church of the East along lines correlating with Roman vs. Sassanian political geography — Sebastian Brock's work on East Syria is the standard reference. Richard Rubenstein (*When Jesus Became God*, 1999) argues Arianism lost at least as much to Theodosius's 380 Edict of Thessalonica as to Athanasian argument. Justinian's Three Chapters maneuver and the Monothelete controversy were transparently imperial. Peter Brown (*The Rise of Western Christendom*) makes the broader case that orthodoxy tracks imperial infrastructure.

**Honest count on ten biggest controversies** (Arian, Nestorian, Monophysite, Pelagian, Donatist, Iconoclast, Filioque, Monothelete, Mu'tazila/Ash'ari, Hesychast): ~3 plausibly decided by argument (Pelagian via Augustine, Hesychast via Palamas at 1351, the long-run Arian settlement once imperial politics stopped swinging), ~5 decided by political force (Chalcedon enforcement, both iconoclast reversals, Filioque as schism marker rather than argument, Monothelete imposed then reversed, mihna), ~2 genuinely mixed (Donatist, Nestorian). That ratio is fatal to a strong darwinian reading.

**Recurrence claim — partially holds but inverts.** Arian-adjacent positions recur (Socinians, Unitarians, JW, early Mormonism). Modalism recurs as Oneness Pentecostalism. Adoptionism recurs in various low-Christology liberal Protestantisms. But the recurring positions are the *losers*, not the winners — which inverts the user's claim. If recurrence indicates cognitive fitness, Arian-adjacent unitarianism is fit. The framework can't have it both ways: either marginal survival counts (and heresies qualify) or it doesn't (and recurrence is irrelevant to the selection argument).

**Framework mapping (flagging forced fits):** Trinity-as-three-persons-one-substance maps weakly to Functor composition — the persons aren't pipeline stages, they're co-eternal. Forced. Chalcedonian two-natures maps better: type union with preserved invariants ("without confusion" = no type collapse; "without separation" = no product-of-disjoint-types; it's a genuine sum-with-shared-identity). The two-natures case is the one non-forced mapping in this angle.

**Dead if applicable:** Not dead. The invariant-preservation frame genuinely illuminates Nicea and Chalcedon. But the darwinian-selection *mechanism* is dead as stated — empire explains too much of the variance. Survivable reformulation: the framework predicts *what kinds of formulations are stable once a tradition commits to them*, not *which formulations win*. Weaker but defensible.

**Open questions:**
- Does the Rabbinic *elu v'elu* pattern contradict the creedal single-winner pattern, suggesting different selection regimes per tradition? (Literacy-without-empire vs. empire-with-literacy.)
- Is there a case where a conciliar decision was later reversed by argument without political change? Iconoclasm's 843 reversal was political (Theodora's regency). I can't think of a clean case. If none exists, that is strong evidence against memetic fitness as driver.
- Does Hesychasm at 1351 count as argument-driven? Byzantine politics were involved but Palamite theology won after Palamas was personally out of favor — worth investigating.

### H5: Confession / consolidation protocol (opus, alive)

**Verdict:** Four independent traditions converge on roughly the same 4-step protocol (recognize → regret → confess/commit → restitute/resolve) and span the full sync-to-batch frequency range. Strong convergence evidence Consolidate is a real cognitive attractor, not a framework artifact. Protestant privatization shows memetic fitness tracks the structure, not only the institution.

**Claims:**

- **Jewish teshuvah (Rambam, *Mishneh Torah*, Hilchot Teshuva 1:1, 2:2):** operative steps are cessation of the sin, verbal confession (*vidui*) before God, regret for the past act, firm resolve not to repeat. Rambam's operational test in 2:1 — *complete* teshuvah is when the person, given the same opportunity and ability, declines — defines success as a change in the action-generating policy, not a change in feeling. Behavioral postcondition, 12th century.

- **Catholic reconciliation (Council of Trent, Session XIV, 1551, *De Sacramento Paenitentiae*; CCC §1422-1498, esp. §1448 "acts of the penitent" and §1451 on contrition):** four named acts — contrition, confession, satisfaction/penance, absolution by the minister. §1451 distinguishes "perfect" contrition (sorrow from love of God) from "imperfect" (fear of punishment) — a precondition-strength check on the input.

- **Buddhist protocols:** (a) Theravāda *pāṭimokkha* — fortnightly *uposatha* recitation of the 227 monastic rules, breaches confessed before the saṅgha (Vinaya Piṭaka, Mahāvagga). (b) Mahāyāna four opponent powers, formalized in Śāntideva's *Bodhicaryāvatāra* ch. 2: reliance, regret, remedy, resolve. (c) Vajrayāna Vajrasattva practice wraps the four powers in visualization and hundred-syllable mantra.

- **Islamic tawba (Qur'an 66:8, *tawbatan naṣūḥan*; al-Ghazali, *Iḥyāʾ ʿUlūm al-Dīn*, Kitāb al-Tawba):** al-Ghazali's conditions — recognition of the wrong, remorse, cessation, and where rights of other persons are involved, restitution (*radd al-maẓālim*) or seeking their forgiveness. The rights-of-God vs rights-of-people split is a type-check on what completes the update.

- **Consolidate mapping (uniform across all four):** the *store* is the memory of acts (violated commitments, episodic traces); the *evaluation* is judgment against the law or precepts (halakha, moral law, Vinaya, sharīʿa); the *parameter update* is a revised disposition producing changed future action. Rambam's operational test and the Buddhist "remedy + resolve" make the parameter-write contract most explicit.

- **Frequency schedule — full sync/batch range:**
  - Synchronous on impasse: Catholic confession required before communion after mortal sin; immediate tawba in hadith literature; monastic confession on breach.
  - Daily batch: Ignatian *examen* (Ignatius, *Spiritual Exercises* §43, a five-point nightly review); Muslim *muḥāsaba*, treated by al-Ghazali in the *Iḥyāʾ* book on muraqaba and muhasaba; Jewish nightly *vidui* before Shema al ha-Mittah.
  - Deep batch: Yom Kippur (Leviticus 16, 23); Lent and Easter confession; Ramadan intensification at Laylat al-Qadr; monsoon *pavāraṇā* closing the rains retreat.

- **Empirical backing (partial).** Desistance research in criminology (Maruna, *Making Good*, 2001) finds ex-offenders who sustain change construct explicit redemption narratives with recognition + commitment structure — same shape, secular container. Twelve-step programs replicate the four-step protocol with daily-batch scheduling in steps 4-10; a 2020 Cochrane review (Kelly et al.) found AA/TSF at least non-inferior to CBT for abstinence outcomes. Evidence the protocol does real work after the institutional container is stripped off.

**Dead if applicable:** n/a — alive, with caveats below.

**Open questions:**

- Scrupulosity and religious-themed OCD (documented in the OCD literature, e.g. Abramowitz & Jacoby 2014) show the protocol running pathologically — Consolidate firing without an impasse-resolution gate. Traditions encode preconditions (sufficient matter, firm purpose, one-rite-per-act) but individuals can bypass them. Is the mapping normative or descriptive?
- Protestant privatization (Luther, *Von der Beicht*, 1521; Calvin, *Institutes* III.4) stripped the priest-minister while preserving the four-step structure prayed directly to God. The protocol survived institutional collapse — argues memetic fitness tracks structure, not power.
- Zen's relative de-emphasis on verbal confession vs. *zazen* is a candidate counterexample worth another pass — does it dispense with the protocol, or relocate the parameter-write into sitting practice?

### H3: Contemplative pipelines (opus, partial)

**Verdict:** Three traditions converge on a "stabilize → discriminate → surrender" shape that maps cleanly to Attend → Filter → Consolidate. The front of the pipeline (Perceive/Cache) and the Transmit role map less cleanly. Evidence is suggestive, not decisive.

**Claims:**

- **Lectio divina (Guigo II, *Scala Claustralium* / *Ladder of Monks*, c. 1150s, 12th-c. Carthusian prior of Grande Chartreuse).** Four rungs: *lectio* (reading Scripture), *meditatio* ("studious insearching with the mind to know what was before concealed"), *oratio* ("devout desiring of the heart to get what is good and avoid what is evil"), *contemplatio* ("lifting up of the heart to God tasting somewhat of the heavenly sweetness"). Mapping: lectio = Perceive; meditatio = Cache+Filter (hold text, discriminate meaning); oratio = Transmit (Guigo explicitly frames it as address across a boundary — to God); contemplatio = Consolidate (grace produces a change the practitioner cannot author). User's read holds.

- **Samatha-vipassanā (Buddhaghosa, *Visuddhimagga*, 5th-c. Theravāda commentarial summa; canonical roots in the *Satipaṭṭhāna* and *Ānāpānasati* suttas).** Samatha develops *parikamma-* → *upacāra-* → *appanā-samādhi* (preparatory, access, absorption concentration) through the four *jhānas*; vipassanā then reflects on those states as marked by the three characteristics (*anicca*, *dukkha*, *anattā*). Mapping: samatha = **Attend** (stabilize on a single object — clean fit); vipassanā = **Filter** (discriminate the three marks in what arises); stream-entry and *nibbāna* = Consolidate (irreversible parameter change — a stream-enterer cannot regress below that floor). Caveat: *upacāra* and *appanā* are Buddhaghosa's commentarial vocabulary, not in the Pali suttas themselves.

- **Hesychasm (John Climacus, *Klimax tou Theiou Anodou* / *Ladder of Divine Ascent*, 7th-c. Sinai; later systematized in the *Philokalia*, compiled by Nikodemos and Makarios, 1782).** Thirty rungs culminating in *apatheia*, *hēsychia*, and *theōria*; the Jesus Prayer anchors the "descent of the mind into the heart." Mapping: the descent = Attend stabilization (shift attention from discursive to somatic locus); *nēpsis* (watchfulness) = Filter (guarding against *logismoi*); *theōria* = Consolidate. Clean fit on the middle stages; the thirty rungs are a virtue-acquisition ladder, not a per-session pipeline — don't overclaim the 30-step structure.

- **Ignatian *Spiritual Exercises* (Ignatius of Loyola, 1548).** Four weeks: sin/mercy, life of Christ, passion, resurrection. Within each, *compositio loci* (imaginative reconstruction of a Gospel scene) then *applicatio sensuum* (application of the five senses to that scene). Mapping: *compositio* = Cache (load the scene into working memory); *applicatio sensuum* = Perceive run against cached content; colloquy = Transmit; the "election" at the end of Week Two (discernment to a concrete life decision) = Consolidate.

- **Convergence (the strong claim).** Lectio divina, samatha-vipassanā, and hesychasm share the shape: (a) stabilize attention on a single object (Scripture word / breath / Jesus prayer), (b) let discriminating content arise and filter it, (c) surrender to a non-authored change. Three traditions, three substrates, same shape. Ignatius breaks the pattern — his pipeline front-loads on Cache (imaginative reconstruction). The attractor is real for apophatic/attention-first traditions but not universal; kataphatic imaginative-prayer lineages fit a different shape.

- **Failure modes.** Zen (*kōan*, *shikantaza*) and Dzogchen *trekchö* explicitly reject staged pipelines — "direct pointing," "already enlightened." This doesn't falsify the pipeline view: they presuppose prior stage-training (Zen monks sit *zazen* before kōan work; Dzogchen requires *ngöndro* preliminaries). The "no-stages" move is pedagogical inside a culture that did the stages. Harder problem: introspective reports of stages are exactly what Nisbett & Wilson's *Telling More Than We Can Know* (1977) warned us to distrust. Practitioners may confabulate stages onto undifferentiated phenomenology their tradition trained them to carve. Cross-tradition convergence partly answers this (confabulation should diverge, not converge), but historical contact via Evagrius and Silk-Road transmission is plausible and would inflate apparent convergence.

- **Memetic fitness vs. empirical grounding.** Lutz, Slagter, Dunne & Davidson, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences* (2008), "Attention Regulation and Monitoring in Meditation," formalized the Focused Attention / Open Monitoring distinction. FA maps to samatha, OM to vipassanā — and matches the Attend/Filter split. Lutz et al., *PNAS* (2004) showed long-term Tibetan practitioners have measurably different gamma coherence and attention-network activity. Weak-to-moderate evidence that the Attend and Filter stages correspond to neurally real operations. No comparable evidence for Transmit or Consolidate — those remain theology-shaped.

**Dead if applicable:** Not dead. Partial: convergence is real on the middle stages across 3/4 traditions; the front and back of the pipeline are weaker; introspection-reliability and cross-tradition contamination remain unresolved threats to the independence claim.

**Open questions:** (1) Did Evagrius Ponticus's eight-thoughts schema mediate Buddhist→Christian pipeline transmission through late-antique Alexandria? If yes, the "independent convergence" argument weakens substantially. (2) Is there a confabulation-resistant way to compare stages — behavioral or neural markers rather than first-person reports? Lutz/Davidson have started this; coverage is thin. (3) Sufi *dhikr* (Qushayri's *Risāla*, 11th-c.; Naqshbandi *latā'if*) and rabbinic *hitbonenut* (systematized by Chabad in the 18th c. — too late to count as independent) were not analyzed here; worth a second pass, especially Sufi.

### H2: Ritual purity codes (opus, partial)

**Verdict:** Purity codes are real, cross-tradition preconditions on ritual action, but they behave as a *layered* computational structure (null op for core rites + degraded op for peripheral ones + scheduled batch reset) rather than a single Perceive-side filter. Structural convergence across traditions is strong; content convergence (what counts as impure) is weak. Reform-era collapses show the layer is compressible without tradition-death — which narrows the "computationally necessary" claim to "computationally common."

**Claims:**
- **Islam (salat).** Qurʾan 5:6 mandates wudu before prayer; ghusl after janaba, menstruation, post-partum; tayammum (clean earth) when water is unavailable (Qurʾan 4:43, 5:6). The hadith "No salat is accepted without purification" is in Sahih Muslim Book of Purification (commonly cited as Muslim 224/225) and referenced in Bukhari 6954. Classical fiqh (al-Shafiʿi's *Risala*; Ibn Rushd's *Bidayat al-Mujtahid*) enumerates and disputes nawaqid al-wudu — urination, defecation, flatulence, deep sleep are agreed; touching the opposite sex and touching genitals are contested across madhhabs. **Op class: null.** Without wudu, salat is batil (invalid), not makruh (disliked) — type error, not soft constraint. (Citations verified via sunnah.com and hadeethenc.com; I have not consulted critical Arabic editions.)
- **Rabbinic Judaism.** Leviticus 11–15 and Numbers 19 enumerate impurity sources; Mishnah Seder Tohorot codifies them in 12 tractates. Kelim 1:1 lists the *avot ha-tumah*. Post-70 CE, most Temple-dependent tumah laws went dormant because the operation (Temple sacrifice) stopped running. What survives in Orthodox practice: niddah (menstrual separation + mikveh immersion), corpse-impurity restrictions on kohanim, netilat yadayim (handwashing) before bread. **Op class: null for Temple rites (no Temple = no op); null for niddah (sexual contact forbidden during); ritualized-hygienic for handwashing.**
- **Hinduism (śuddhi / āśauca).** Manusmṛti 5.57–5.104 sets āśauca periods — ten days of sūtaka (birth) and mṛtaka (death) impurity for sapindas, with varna-graded variants. Other dharmaśāstras (Yājñavalkya, Parāśara) give different counts, so "ten days" is not canonical across the tradition. Daily purity: ācamana (sipping water), snāna (bathing) before pūjā. Patrick Olivelle's work on the Dharmasūtras (1999) notes the unresolved tension between pollution-as-kinship-marker and pollution-as-bodily-substance; the literature is contested and I should not pick a side. **Op class: mixed.** Temple entry during āśauca is forbidden (null); private pūjā without ācamana is degraded.
- **Shinto (kegare / harae).** Death, blood, disease are principal kegare. Harae subtypes: misogi (water immersion), ōharae (biannual national purification, codified in the *Engishiki*, 927 CE), shubatsu (salt sprinkling). Tsumi splits into amatsutsumi (offenses against kami) and kunitsutsumi (offenses against community). Still practiced: temizu at shrine entry, roughly 50-day mourning-period shrine avoidance. **Op class: entry-gated (null at shrine) + scheduled batch (ōharae in 6th and 12th months).**

**Precondition → operation → postcondition mapping.** Across all four: purity is a precondition on *actor* (body, mental state) and often *material/space* (water, earth, shrine boundary, vessel). Violation invalidates core ritual acts (salat, Temple sacrifice, temple entry, niddah) — null op. Violation degrades peripheral acts (private supplication, Torah study, household prayer) — unworthy op. "Purity = type check" holds for a narrow subset and overreaches as a universal.

**Natural Framework mapping — revised from the brief.** Purity-before-ritual is Perceive/Filter-side gating on a single cycle (agrees with brief). But *scheduled* resets — weekly Sabbath, annual Yom Kippur, lunar Ramadan, biannual ōharae, monthly niddah, annual śrāddha — behave like Consolidate-phase windows: batch reset of accumulated state. Two distinct computational roles wearing one vocabulary. Worth splitting in the main post: **single-cycle purity = Filter; scheduled purity = Consolidate window.** Overlaps with H5's Consolidate claim — cross-reference.

**Cross-tradition convergence — adversarial.** The hygiene-hypothesis rival is live. Mary Douglas (*Purity and Danger*, 1966) rejected it; Marvin Harris's cultural materialism (1974) revived a functionalist reading; Valerie Curtis and Adam Biran (2001, *Perspectives in Biology and Medicine*) argue disgust-as-pathogen-avoidance drives much of it. This credibly explains corpse/blood/effluent avoidance; it does *not* explain Sabbath scheduling or ōharae. Douglas's own thesis — dirt = matter out of place, purity = classification maintenance — lines up better with a computational reading (boundary enforcement on a symbolic system). I am not claiming Douglas proves computational theology; her framework is consistent with it. The scholarly debate is not settled.

**Dead if applicable:** Not dead, weakened. Reform Judaism's 1885 Pittsburgh Platform §4 explicitly revoked "Mosaic and rabbinical laws regulating diet, priestly purity, and dress"; Reform persists 140 years later. Protestant Reformation stripped Catholic purity structures (confession-before-eucharist discipline, fasting rules) and Protestantism did not die. So the purity layer is **compressible without tradition-death** — computationally common, not computationally necessary. Rebuttal: Reform and Protestant movements may be running on borrowed purity-infrastructure from the parent tradition and could decay faster in isolation; untestable at the millennial timescales the hypothesis invokes.

**Open questions:**
- Is the null/degraded split statable as a theorem? H1's sacramental-validity work should settle the null case.
- Are scheduled purity cycles better mapped to Consolidate than Perceive/Filter? Intersects H5.
- Is Douglas's classification-maintenance thesis formally equivalent to type-checking? Needs a close read of *Purity and Danger* ch. 2–3.
- Collapse evidence is from movements ≤200 years old. Cannot yet tell whether Reform survives without the purity substrate at millennial timescales.

## Cycle 2 — falsification tests

User note: 1000 years of prayer is long but not forever; expect disputes and inaccuracies at the tradition-internal level. Focus cycle 2 on structural falsifiers, not citation-hunting. Guard against confirmation bias.

### C2-A: H5 adversarial edge cases (opus, narrows)

**Verdict:** Four of five cases relocate rather than drop the protocol, but Lutheran *sola fide* structurally gutted one step (satisfaction) and Pure Land/Shin inverts the agency of the whole pipeline. H5 survives only if reformulated as "the shape is invariant up to substitution of the efficacious agent" — the original entry overclaimed because it treated "four named acts" as the invariant, when the real invariant is weaker: a recognize → respond → alter-future-disposition skeleton that every tradition must instantiate, with wildly different actors driving it.

**Five test cases:**

1. **Sōtō Zen — relocates.** Dōgen's *Shushōgi* (compiled 1890 from *Shōbōgenzō* extracts by Ouchi Seiran and Takiya Takushū — modernist Sōtō, flagged; not Dōgen-direct) opens with *sange metsuzai* (repentance eliminating evil karma) as the first of four householder principles, followed by *jukai*, vow-generation, and *gyōji hōon*. The standard Zen gatha — "All the evil karma ever committed by me since of old, on account of beginningless greed, hatred, and delusion, born of my body, speech, and thought, I now repent all of it" (recited at *fusatsu* and *jukai*) — compresses recognize + regret + confess into one triplet; the *jukai* vow itself performs resolve/restitute. Dōgen in *Shōbōgenzō Keisei-sanshoku* treats repentance as enabling practice, not earning forgiveness. Structure preserved, theology inverted (no external absolver).

2. **Jōdo Shinshū — breaks the "four acts" claim.** Shinran in the *Tannishō* (recorded posthumously by Yuien-bō) and in *Kyōgyōshinshō* treats *jiriki* self-authored repentance as spiritually useless: the moral quality of *shinjin* (entrusting) is Amida's working, not the practitioner's. Yet *ki no jinshin* — "the deep mind concerning self," inherited from Shan-tao — is explicit recognition of oneself as a foolish being of blind passions, and Shinran in the *Mattōshō* tells correspondents to "deeply repent." Recognize and regret survive as constitutive of *shinjin*; the confess/resolve/restitute arc is assigned to Amida's completed work rather than the practitioner's effort. Not a compressed four-step — a bifurcated one where steps 1-2 stay human and steps 3-4 are outsourced. This breaks the original H5 claim that the *four acts* converge; only the front half converges.

3. **Lutheran *sola fide* — narrows the protocol.** Augsburg Confession XII (1530) defines repentance as two parts — contrition and faith — not three or four. The Apology's Art. XIIb (Melanchthon) explicitly rejects satisfaction as a condition of forgiveness; good works follow as fruit, not completion condition. This is exactly the dropped step the H5 entry should have flagged. Luther in *Von der Beicht* kept private confession as pastoral practice and retained absolution as comfort; he stripped the Catholic four-slot schema to two. The original H5 entry's claim that "Protestant privatization preserved the four-step structure" is wrong for confessional Lutheranism. Restitution survives ethically (neighbor-love) but is decoupled from the Consolidate contract — it does not complete the parameter-write, faith does.

4. **Pentecostal altar call — temporally compresses.** Azusa Street (1906–15) and the Wesleyan-Holiness altar-tradition it inherited from Phoebe Palmer's "altar theology" (1830s–40s) feature two scheduled operations: the conviction/confession event at the altar rail, and the tarrying room for baptism in the Holy Spirit. Recognize (conviction under preaching), regret (public weeping), confess (spoken at altar or in tarrying), resolve (testimony and walk) all occur but serialized into minutes. Charles Parham and William Seymour distinguished justification, sanctification, and Spirit-baptism as separable crisis events, so the ordo is still staged at *life* scale even when compressed at *event* scale. This is the "synchronous sub-second Consolidate" case the framework predicts: structure preserved, serialization radically sped up. Does not break H5.

5. **Aghori / left-hand Tantra — subverts H2, not H5.** Aghori practice at Manikarnika and in the Kina Ram lineage inverts the *purity* side (H2) by charnel-ground eating, but the internal training retains a recognize-regret-resolve arc around attachment and aversion; Vajrayāna Vajrasattva samaya-repair via the four opponent powers (reliance, regret, remedy, resolve) is practiced alongside transgressive tsog. The transgression targets the purity precondition, not the Consolidate protocol itself. Structure preserved, H2 subverted, H5 intact.

**Score:** 1 of 5 breaks the four-step claim (Shin Pure Land); 1 of 5 narrows it structurally (Lutheran drops satisfaction); 3 of 5 relocate or compress while preserving the shape (Zen, Pentecostal, Tantra).

**Consequence for H5:** The original entry must be rewritten. Two fixes:
- Drop the "Protestant privatization preserved the four-step" line — true-ish for Calvin's *Institutes* III.4 (mortification, vivification, confession without Catholic satisfaction), false for confessional Lutheranism which formally went to two parts. Either cite Calvin specifically or pull the claim.
- Weaken the invariant. Cross-tradition convergence is on a *skeleton* (recognition of wrong → affective response → altered disposition), not on four named acts. Shin bifurcates agency; Luther drops satisfaction. The "four independent traditions converge on the same 4-step" framing is Abrahamic-plus-monastic-Buddhist and fails on the two largest East Asian Buddhist movements (Jōdo Shinshū and Jōdo-shū together are the largest bloc of Japanese Buddhist affiliation) and on 70M+ confessional Lutherans worldwide.

**Open questions:**
- Is Calvin's three-part repentance closer to the Catholic four than Luther's two? One more pass before rewriting H5.
- Does Shin's bifurcation falsify Consolidate-as-repentance, or extend the framework to "Consolidate can be driven by an upstream agent, the practitioner being a downstream reader"? The second reading would be a substantive extension, not a save.
- *Shushōgi* is 1890, not Dōgen. Pre-modern Sōtō repentance liturgy (Keizan's *Denkōroku*, medieval Eihei-ji *fusatsu*) should be checked before claiming structural preservation across the whole Sōtō arc.

### C2-B: Triviality test (opus, preferential-fit)

**Verdict:** Common law and peer review fit the six-role frame about as tightly as H1/H5 theology and more explicitly in places; Go fits the middle tightly but the Consolidate cell is thinner. Codex's "any norm-governed symbolic system maps" dismissal is substantially vindicated. Theology's residual advantage is narrow: it names the *minister/Transmit* role and the *in-persona* constraint more explicitly than the secular systems do — insufficient to carry the memetic-fitness claim on its own.

**System 1 — English common law:** Hart's rule of recognition (*The Concept of Law*, 1961) is native type-enforcement: a norm counts as law iff certifiable by the recognition rule. Rules of evidence (hearsay, relevance; *Federal Rules* 401/802 in the US tradition) are explicit Filter preconditions with documented invalidation clauses — closer to a strict type-checker than sacramental "intent," because admissibility is decidable at a voir dire. Stare decisis is Cache/Transmit across time; ratio/dicta is the Filter selecting what binds. Jury instructions are Attend control. Appeal plus *overruling* is a clean Consolidate backward pass: the appellate ruling reads the record and writes a parameter change to the legal corpus, distinct from caching the new case. Equity (Maitland's *Equity*) is a runtime override structurally parallel to Orthodox *oikonomia*. Full six-role coverage; preconditions and postconditions are explicit in pleading standards and judgment entry. Fit is **tighter than H1** on decidability, comparable on structure.

**System 2 — Peer review:** Submission guidelines (word limits, data availability, ethics clearance) are matter/form preconditions, enforced by desk rejection — a literal Filter fire. Merton's CUDOS norms supply the type-enforcement layer, though descriptive-normative rather than enforced. Reviewer expertise is the minister-analog; conflict-of-interest rules are the "valid minister" check. Acceptance criteria (novel, correct, significant) are a typed postcondition. Replication (Ioannidis 2005, *PLoS Medicine*) is postcondition verification across time. Retraction (COPE guidelines; Retraction Watch as audit) is an unambiguous Consolidate backward pass: record rewritten, citation behavior updates, sometimes tenure cases reopen. Kuhn is batch Consolidate at longer timescale; Popperian falsification is the per-submission contract. Full six-role coverage, preconditions explicit, Consolidate clearly separate from Cache. Fit is **equal to H5** and arguably tighter on Consolidate — retraction is a dated event with a written parameter update (CrossRef retraction notice), an audit trail no theology cited here matches.

**System 3 — Go:** Rules (Japanese, Chinese, Ing; Nihon Ki-in rules 1989) are type enforcement; legal-move predicates (no suicide, ko, board bounds) are preconditions checked per move. Joseki (Ishida's *Dictionary of Basic Joseki*, 1977) is a cached local-pattern library — Cache is exceptionally explicit, more than in theology. "Reading" is Attend/Filter in tandem. Referees handle disputed positions (triple ko, long cycle) — runtime override akin to equity. Dan promotion via tournament record is Consolidate: the Nihon Ki-in reads results and writes a rank parameter. Two cells run thinner than theology: Transmit is generic (*kifu* game records) with no *in-persona* constraint, and Consolidate operates on the *player*, not the *joseki corpus* — post-AlphaGo 3-3 invasions (ca. 2017) spread by tournament imitation without a governing body. Fit is **tighter on Cache, looser on Transmit and corpus-level Consolidate** than theology.

**Comparative ranking (tightest first):** peer review ≈ common law > H5 > H1 ≈ Go > H3 > H4 > H2. The two secular bureaucratic systems occupy the top tier. Theology's only structural advantage is explicit naming of the minister/Transmit role with an *in-persona* boundary — common law's "officer of the court" is close but weaker; peer review's "reviewer" has no comparable ontological commitment.

**Consequence for the hypothesis:** Codex's dismissal mostly stands. The six-role frame maps onto any norm-governed symbolic system with a feedback loop — roughly what "durable institution" means. Theology's fit is not vacuously equal — minister/Transmit explicitness is a real residual — but not preferential enough to carry memetic-selection-from-correctness. Honest post-cycle-2 claim: *the framework describes the class of durable norm-governed systems*, of which theology is one well-documented instance. The stronger claim that theology uniquely encodes cognitive correctness is unsupported by this test. C2-C's pre-registered prediction is the remaining load-bearing test; if scheduled batch Consolidate is also universal across non-theological durables (fiscal year-end close, season-end rank recalculation, annual peer-review cycles), even that prediction weakens to "feature of durable norm-governed systems generally."

### C2-C: Predictive test — scheduled Consolidate universality (opus, partial)

**Prediction (pre-registered above):** Any durable religious/wisdom tradition > ~1M adherents, > 500 years old, has (a) a scheduled annual/seasonal collective repentance/review rite AND (b) a daily or near-daily individual examination-of-conscience practice. Both required for full confirmation.

**Six-tradition tests (sources flagged; web-verified secondary, not manuscript-level):**

1. **Jainism** — **Batch: yes.** Paryushana/Daslakshana (8–10 days) culminates in *Samvatsari Pratikramana* — a collective, calendar-fixed rite of asking forgiveness from every living being, with the phrase *Micchāmi Dukkaḍam* ("may my wrong be inconsequential"). **Daily: yes.** *Pratikramana* is one of the six *āvaśyaka* (essential daily duties); ideally twice daily (*raishi* at dawn, *devasi* at dusk) in Śvetāmbara monastic practice, once or fortnightly for most laity. Source: Padmanabh Jaini, *The Jaina Path of Purification* (1979), ch. on *āvaśyaka*; corroborated by Wikipedia/Jain rituals entry. **Verdict: fully confirmed.** Surprise: the fortnightly (*pakkhi*) and four-month (*chaumasi*) intermediate tiers give Jainism *four* nested review cadences (daily, fortnightly, seasonal, annual) — a finer-grained schedule than any H5 tradition.

2. **Zoroastrianism** — **Batch: yes.** *Muktad/Farvardegan* (10 days at year-end) combines ancestor honoring with *Patet* recitation. *Pateti* is the dedicated day-of-repentance at year's turn. **Daily: yes.** *Patet Pashemani* (Pazand repentance prayer, attributed to Dastur Adarbad Mahraspand, Sasanian era) is part of *Khordeh Avesta* daily devotions; a short substitute is embedded in the *Kasti* ritual, which is tied five times daily (*Gāhs*). Source: Mary Boyce, *Zoroastrians: Their Religious Beliefs and Practices* (1979); corroborated by Karanjia's prayer exposition (ramiyarkaranjia.com). **Verdict: fully confirmed.** Surprise: the *Kasti* rite binds repentance to a physical action (untying and retying the sacred cord) at each prayer-gāh — the most embodied daily Consolidate trigger in the sample.

3. **Sikhism** — **Batch: partial/weak.** Vaisakhi, Gurpurabs, and Hola Mohalla are foundational/commemorative, not primarily penitential. There is no Sikh *Yom-Kippur-analog*. **Daily: yes, but fused.** *Ardas* (recited after morning/evening *Nitnem* and before tasks) contains an explicit petition asking Vaheguru to forgive daily sins (*bhul-chuk baksh lainī*); this is the classical "third section" of the Ardas per *Sikh Rehat Maryada*. Guarding against confirmation bias: the daily forgiveness petition is real and explicit (not "all prayer is implicit repentance"), but the scheduled *batch* cell is genuinely thin — closest thing is periodic *Akhand Path* continuous-reading vigils, which are devotional rather than penitential. Source: *Sikh Rehat Maryada* (SGPC, 1945); Nikky-Guninder Kaur Singh, *The Birth of the Khalsa* (2005) for Khalsa formation. **Verdict: partially confirmed (daily yes, annual no).**

4. **Confucian ritual tradition** — **Batch: weak/no.** Qingming, *Zhongyuan* (ghost festival), and *Hanyi* are ancestor-honoring and filial-piety rites, not collective *repentance*. Imperial *Jiao* sacrifices occasionally included confessional elements on behalf of the realm, but for the lay tradition the annual cadence is veneration, not review. **Daily: yes.** *Analects* 1.4: Zengzi's *sān xǐng wú shēn* — "I daily examine myself on three points: in transacting business for others, was I not faithful? In friendship, was I not sincere? Have I not practiced what was transmitted?" (Legge trans.) Neo-Confucian Song-era texts (Zhu Xi's *Family Rituals*; *Jinsi lu*) formalize *jìng* (reverent attention) as continuous self-monitoring. Source: *Analects* 1.4; Zhu Xi, *Jiali*. **Verdict: partially confirmed (daily yes, annual-repentance no — ancestor rites do a different job).** Surprise: the Confucian daily practice is the *most textually explicit* in the sample (a numbered checklist, 5th c. BCE), yet the tradition never developed the collective-repentance complement — a clean structural asymmetry.

5. **Bahá'í Faith** — **Batch: yes.** Nineteen-Day Fast (month of *‘Alá’*, sunrise-to-sunset, March 2–20), preceded by *Ayyám-i-Há* (intercalary charity days) — explicitly framed as a period of spiritual renewal and self-examination. **Daily: yes.** Obligatory Prayer (short, medium, or long form — Bahá'u'lláh's choice-of-three) plus the *Tablet of Ahmad* and *Hidden Words* for reflection; self-examination is explicitly enjoined ("Bring thyself to account each day ere thou art summoned to a reckoning," *Arabic Hidden Words* #31). Source: *Kitáb-i-Aqdas* ¶10, ¶16; Bahá'u'lláh, *The Hidden Words*. **Verdict: fully confirmed.** Surprise: the "bring thyself to account each day" injunction is the *cleanest* first-person self-examination imperative in any scripture I checked — closer to the framework's Consolidate-contract wording than anything in Rambam.

6. **Mandaean / Yazidi** — **Mandaean skipped:** current population ~14,000 globally (Kurdistan24, 2023) — three orders of magnitude below the 1M pre-registered threshold, so the tradition falls outside the prediction's scope. **Yazidi substitute: partial.** Çarşema Sor (Red Wednesday, early April) is a creation-renewal festival, not primarily penitential. Forty-day summer/winter fasts (*Çilê Havînê*, *Çilê Zivistanê*) and the three-day fast before *Îda Êzî* exist, but published sources (Wikipedia, Kurdish Center for Studies) frame these as ascetic/cosmological rather than review-of-conduct. Daily individual examination practice: I could not verify an explicit self-examination rite in available sources — Yazidi liturgy is oral and caste-mediated (by *Pîr* and *Sheikh*), so absence in public sources is not strong evidence of absence. **Verdict: unverified / likely partial.** Flag: Yazidi population estimates (~700K–1M) sit at the threshold; treat as borderline in-scope. Honest mark: *unverified*, not a clean data point.

**Score:** 3/6 fully confirmed (Jainism, Zoroastrianism, Bahá'í); 2/6 partially confirmed (Sikhism lacks annual rite; Confucianism lacks annual *repentance* rite); 1/6 unverified (Yazidi). **Zero clean falsifications, but also only half the traditions give full confirmation.**

**Surprises / anomalies:**
- Jainism's *four* nested cadences (daily, fortnightly, seasonal, annual) — the framework's binary "synchronous + batch" taxonomy is too coarse; there's a continuum.
- Bahá'u'lláh's "bring thyself to account each day" is a tighter Consolidate-contract specification than Rambam's postcondition, and only ~150 years old — the pattern is still being invented, not just preserved.
- Two of the six traditions (Sikhism, Confucianism) have strong daily review but no annual collective-repentance rite. This is the prediction's first real leak.
- Confucianism's asymmetry is structurally interesting: the tradition developed the *synchronous-on-impasse* mode (Zengzi) but routed "batch" energy into ancestor veneration rather than collective review. If Consolidate were a cognitive universal, we'd expect pressure toward filling the annual-repentance cell; its absence over 2500 years is evidence the cell is *optional*, not obligatory.

**Verdict on prediction:** **Partially supported, not confirmed.** The "daily individual review" leg holds in 5/5 verifiable cases. The "scheduled annual collective repentance" leg holds in only 3/5. The framework's claim should narrow to: *daily individual Consolidate is near-universal in durable traditions; scheduled collective Consolidate is common but not obligatory.* Combined with C2-B's finding that secular bureaucracies exhibit the same pattern (fiscal close, retraction, rank recalculation), the residual claim is weaker still: scheduled Consolidate is a feature of *most* durable norm-governed systems, with genuine exceptions on the collective-repentance axis. Not a cognitive universal; a strong attractor.

### C2-D: Case-control on dead traditions (opus, H0 with one clean H2)

**Pre-registered H0:** Losers had full six-role coverage; survival not predicted by framework fit. **H1:** Losers had role-level deficits. **H2:** Losers failed specifically on Transmit.

**Four pairings:**

1. **Valentinianism vs. Nicene (2nd–3rd c. Mediterranean).** Self-voice: *Gospel of Truth*, *Tripartite Tractate*, *Gospel of Philip* (Nag Hammadi NHC I, XI, II). Winner-filter: Irenaeus *Adversus Haereses* I. Pleromatic cosmology (Perceive); aeon genealogies, 30-aeon Ptolemaic system (Cache); initiation-tiered teaching and ruled allegoresis — Ismo Dunderberg (*Beyond Gnosticism*, 2008) treats Valentinian schools as teacher-disciple scholai (Filter); sustained Pauline/Johannine exegesis (Attend); text production — Nag Hammadi proves it (Transmit); *gnosis* as one-shot transformative write (Consolidate). **Full coverage.** Death: no episcopal infrastructure — they remained schools *inside* Christian congregations rather than building rival hierarchies (Einar Thomassen, *The Spiritual Seed*, 2006). Nicene heresiology plus post-Constantinian legal pressure expelled them. Supports **H0**. *Null check:* the no-rival-hierarchy stance is strategic, not a role absence.

2. **Mohism vs. Confucianism (Warring States, 5th–3rd c. BCE).** Self-voice: *Mozi* (71 chapters extant, preserved via Daoist canon). *Sānbiǎo* three standards — sage-kings, common testimony, utility — the most explicit Perceive contract in pre-Qin thought; *Mozi* plus Later Mohist Canons *Jing* and *Jing Shuo* systematizing logic and proto-science (Cache; A.C. Graham, *Later Mohist Logic, Ethics and Science*, 1978; Chris Fraser, *The Philosophy of the Mòzǐ*, 2016); disputation rules (*bian*), definitions, fallacy taxonomies — sharper than the *Analects* (Filter); *jiān'ài*, anti-fatalism, anti-aggression (Attend); organized *juzi* lineage with designated succession, Mohist defensive-siege engineers for hire (Transmit); communal discipline toward *li* (Consolidate). **Full coverage, tighter Filter than the winner.** Death: Qin unification dissolved the inter-state market; Han imperial Confucianism under Dong Zhongshu (c. 134 BCE) captured the niche; anti-aristocratic stance unusable to a dynastic state. Supports **H0** strongly — the loser had the *better* Filter.

3. **Manichaeism vs. Christianity / Zoroastrianism (3rd–14th c.).** Self-voice: *Cologne Mani Codex*, Coptic *Kephalaia*, Turfan fragments, *Xuastvanift* (Uyghur confession manual). Samuel Lieu (*Manichaeism in the Later Roman Empire and Medieval China*, 2nd ed. 1992). Dualist cosmology (Perceive); Mani authored seven canonical works *himself*, Turfan monasteries kept scriptoria (Cache); Electi/Auditores two-tier membership with food/sex/labor type-gates (Filter); Electi's light-extraction meal ritual (Attend); hierarchical church — Archegos, Magistri, Bishops, Presbyters (Transmit); weekly *Xuastvanift* — one of the most explicit Consolidate contracts in ancient religion, pre-dating the Catholic four-part schema. **Full coverage, tighter Consolidate than contemporaries.** Death: tri-continental persecution — Diocletian 302, Theodosian laws, Mani executed c. 277, Abbasid *zandaqa* inquisitions, Tang edict 843, Uyghur khanate collapse 840. Outlawed everywhere it went. Supports **H0** cleanly.

4. **Mithraism vs. Christianity (2nd–4th c. Roman).** Self-voice: **none surviving in text.** Archaeology only — mithraea, tauroctony reliefs, Santa Prisca/Ostia initiation graffiti, hostile mentions in Justin and Tertullian. Manfred Clauss (*The Roman Cult of Mithras*, 2000) and Roger Beck (*The Religion of the Mithras Cult in the Roman Empire*, 2006) reconstruct; Beck reads the tauroctony as astronomical/soteriological Cache in iconographic form. Cosmic-astronomical frame (Perceive); iconographic Cache, no scripture, oral liturgy; seven-grade initiation — Corax, Nymphus, Miles, Leo, Perses, Heliodromus, Pater (Filter); ritual meal and ordeals (Attend); **Transmit = the failure cell** — no canonical text, cells capped ~30–40 per mithraeum (Clauss), male-only, military-heavy, no cross-language translation; grade advancement as staged parameter-write inaccessible to non-initiates (Consolidate). **Transmit failure.** Post-Theodosian anti-pagan laws (391–392) met a tradition with no portable text to smuggle, no household half-membership, no monastic Cache. Supports **H2 cleanly**. *Null check:* Eleusinian mysteries (also textless) lasted ~2000 years before Theodosian closure — political pressure alone isn't sufficient, but once it arrives, textless cults cannot regenerate. Transmit-deficit is load-bearing *under persecution*, latent otherwise.

**Score:** **H0: 3/4** (Valentinianism, Mohism, Manichaeism). **H2: 1/4 clean** (Mithraism). **H1: 0/4** — no non-Transmit deficit.

**Surprises:**
- The Later Mohist Canons are *more formalized* than Confucian disputation. The loser had the better Filter.
- The *Xuastvanift* is an earlier, tighter Consolidate than the Catholic schema H5 celebrated. The winner was not the best-Consolidating tradition in its niche.
- Nag Hammadi survived c. 4th c. *inside* a Christian monastic library. Losers' Cache preserved by the winner's Transmit — institutional Transmit beats doctrinal Transmit.

**Verdict:** **H0 with one clean H2 exception.** Three losers had full coverage — one with sharper Filter, another with tighter Consolidate than its winner — and died because emperors, caliphs, khans, or ministers shifted patronage. Framework fit did not predict survival; political infrastructure did. Mithraism rescues H2 narrowly under persecution: textless secrecy-cults cannot regenerate once the state turns.

**What this means for the original hypothesis:** The strong form — "survivors won because they fit the framework" — is falsified. Mohism's Filter was sharper than Confucianism's; Manichaeism's Consolidate tighter than Catholicism's; Valentinians matched the Nicenes who expelled them. With C2-B (secular bureaucracies fit as tightly) and C2-C (scheduled Consolidate common but not obligatory), the honest claim collapses to: *the framework describes the class of durable norm-governed systems; within that class, survival tracks political infrastructure.* Narrow residual: under sustained persecution, Transmit-deficit predicts death. Closer to "scripture is load-bearing under stress" than to memetic selection from cognitive correctness.

**Consequence for the overall fan-out hypothesis:** Prayer-level replication of C2-D. Dead Akkadian, Egyptian, and Hermetic prayers encode all six roles as cleanly as the Lord's Prayer or al-Fatiha. The traditions died because Akkad fell, Egypt was Christianized, Ugarit burned, and the Hermetica was absorbed into Christian Neoplatonism — not because the prayers failed cognitively. Together with C2-A, C2-B, C2-C, and C2-D, the survivorship-bias objection is now confirmed at the prayer level. Framework fit is a property of the prayer genre, not a discriminator of which prayers survived. Mithraism (C2-D) remains the only clean structural-failure case across cycle 2 — Transmit-deficit under persecution.
