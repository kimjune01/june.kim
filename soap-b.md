# SOAP Note — BioCryst Pharmaceuticals (BCRX)

**Subject**: BCX7353 (berotralstat / Orladeyo) Phase 3 APeX-2 results posting, NCT03485911

## S — Subjective

BioCryst is a small-cap orphan-drug company that bet its pipeline on BCX7353, an oral once-daily plasma kallikrein inhibitor for hereditary angioedema (HAE) prophylaxis. The company entered Phase 3 with a non-obvious dose selection lesson from Phase 2 — the 250 mg dose failed while 125 mg worked, suggesting a tolerability ceiling that the team acknowledged and wrote back into the pivotal design. By the catalyst date the drug is already FDA-approved (Dec 2020) and commercially launched, making the ClinicalTrials.gov results posting a confirmatory disclosure rather than a gating event. The temporal arc is a company that stumbled on dose-response, repaired its program design, endured a lukewarm topline reception (44% attack reduction vs injectable competitors), but pressed through regulatory and commercial milestones without interruption.

## A — Assessment

**Trajectory category**: living_well

The temporal graph shows a system that absorbed its worst shock (non-monotonic Phase 2 dose-response) early, wrote the learning back into the pivotal design, and then ran a clean regulatory-to-commercial arc from 2019 through approval. The single recurrence of stress at topline (Event 6) was market-perception trauma, not a program-design failure — the primary endpoint was met. The company's response (formulation refinement, commercial buildout, sustained OLE data) converted that stress back to functional within one quarter. By the catalyst date, the drug is approved and launched. The ClinicalTrials.gov results posting is a formalization of data the FDA already reviewed and accepted. There is no mechanistic path by which a results posting contradicts an existing approval.

**Trauma recurrence**: One episode (topline perception shock, May 2019). Resolved. No recurrence through approval.
