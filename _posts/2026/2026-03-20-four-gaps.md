*[MOSAIC](https://arxiv.org/abs/2405.05905) (Duetting et al., 2024) is mechanism-design-complete but market-design-incomplete. A rebuttal. Part of the [Vector Space](/vector-space) series.*

## The money problem

Every chatbot company is losing money on free users. OpenAI projects [$14 billion in inference costs](https://web.archive.org/web/20260227151900/https://finance.yahoo.com/news/openais-own-forecast-predicts-14-150445813.html) for 2026. Subscriptions have a ceiling. Historically, advertising is the business model that scales with free usage.

Perplexity tried first-party ads, earned [$20K total](https://digiday.com/media/perplexitys-ad-business-hasnt-exactly-been-a-hit/), and [killed them](/perplexity-was-right-to-kill-ads). Anthropic [vowed never to run ads in Claude](https://web.archive.org/web/20260217041818/https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/anthropic-no-ads-claude-chatbot-openai-chatgpt.html). OpenAI launched ads at [$60 CPM](https://web.archive.org/web/20260217041818/https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/04/anthropic-no-ads-claude-chatbot-openai-chatgpt.html) with closed targeting logic no one outside OpenAI can audit. Three companies, three strategies, zero consensus on how to do this without destroying trust.

MOSAIC is the best paper on LLM ad auctions I've found. The platform samples M candidate responses from a generation LLM, then reweights them by advertiser reward functions r_i(x,y) — each advertiser's measure of how well a response serves their interests. A KL penalty τ against a reference LLM keeps the output from drifting too far from what the user would have gotten without ads. Rochet (1987) payments enforce strategyproofness via cyclic monotonicity (Theorem 5.1). Four gaps sit between the theory and any market that would adopt it.

## 1. The chatbot selects the ad

MOSAIC's Algorithm 1 runs inside the chatbot: sample candidates from π_gen, score them by Σ r_i(x,y), return y from a softmax over aggregate advertiser reward. The chatbot becomes both the user's agent and the advertiser's agent. This dual agency is the failure mode Perplexity discovered when it [killed its ad business](/perplexity-was-right-to-kill-ads) after $20K in total revenue.

The conflict lives in *who runs the selection*. MOSAIC's mechanism is incentive-compatible for advertisers. It says nothing about who the user trusts to run it.

## 2. τ is not free

MOSAIC's objective:

> J(π) = E[Σ r_i(x,y)] − τ D_KL(π ‖ π_ref)

Maximize aggregate advertiser reward minus a KL penalty against the reference LLM. The paper says τ "enables the auctioneer to balance producing replies closer to the reference LLM or with higher reward for the advertisers." It treats τ as exogenous — a knob the platform turns. It isn't free.

I [swept the equivalent parameter](/the-price-of-relevance) in embedding-space auctions (the log base maps directly to τ) and the tradeoff is monotonic: higher relevance weight improves user outcomes at the cost of revenue, no free lunch. The curve is smooth enough to pick a point, but the paper doesn't model what constrains that choice.

User participation elasticity binds τ from below. If the platform sets τ too low, responses drift too far and users switch platforms. [Gomes (2014)](https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/randje/v45y2014i2p248-272.html) formalized this for two-sided markets: the optimal quality weight is always above the pure revenue-maximizer's choice because the platform has to retain the demand side. The viable region for τ may be narrow enough to collapse the revenue model entirely. The paper doesn't ask.

## 3. Neither party gets receipts

The advertiser pays for influence on the response and can't verify the output was actually steered. The user can't verify it wasn't. This ambiguity is where ad fraud lives — [$84 billion per year](https://www.juniperresearch.com/research/fintech-payments/fraud-identity/ad-fraud-lost-to-fraud/) by Juniper Research's estimate.

Theorem 5.1 proves that truthful reporting is a dominant strategy for each advertiser under Rochet payments. A truthful auction is not the same as a verifiable auction. Strategyproofness only covers the bidding side. It doesn't address whether the mechanism *actually ran*. Did the platform apply the published τ? Did it use the advertiser's reward function or a modified one? Was the KL penalty quietly relaxed?

## 4. The auction has no bouncer

A dropshipper and a twenty-year plumber both submit reward functions. MOSAIC treats them identically. Definition 3.1 guarantees that truthful reporting is dominant "no matter what the others do." But it only constrains *what* advertisers report, not *who* gets to report. Strategyproofness optimizes the wrong margin — honest bidding by unvetted participants.

Without vetting, the mechanism is incentive-compatible for liars and plumbers alike.

## The missing player

MOSAIC models three players: the platform, the advertisers, and the mechanism. It doesn't model the user. [Switching is a few clicks away](/three-levers). If OpenAI pushes τ too low — too aggressive, too personal — users leave for the platform that doesn't steer its answers.

A captive-user assumption is fine for a theory paper. For a market where users can multi-home across ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini in the same browser session, it's the assumption that breaks first.

## Closing the gaps

Each gap has an answer, and they all start from the same place: model the user as a player who can walk away.

Externalize the auction into a [TEE enclave](/perplexity-was-right-to-kill-ads) so the chatbot never selects the ad. Run the [scoring function](/power-diagrams-ad-auctions) in attested hardware where the query embedding never leaves. Let [cryptographic attestation](/attested-attribution) prove the mechanism actually ran with the declared parameters. Build a [trust layer](/proof-of-trust) outside the mechanism so a twenty-year plumber and a yesterday's dropshipper aren't treated the same.

These are governance layers, not auction layers. MOSAIC solves truthful bidding inside the mechanism. Adoption depends on trust outside it. A mechanism that models some of the players and assumes the rest is not market design. It's a theorem looking for a market.
