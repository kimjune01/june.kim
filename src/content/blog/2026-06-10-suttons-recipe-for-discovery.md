On May 12 2026, Richard Sutton recorded a talk for the SAIR workshop on Science for AI. His claim is that generative AI cannot make discoveries, and the reason is mechanical. He opens with a reviewer's joke:

> This work is both novel and good. Unfortunately, the parts that are good are not novel, and the parts that are novel are not good.

That, he says, is generative AI: a mimic whose output is novel, from randomness, or good, from its data, but never both at once.

For science and mathematics, mimicry is fatal, because those need discovery, and discovery has three steps:

> the essence of discovery is to combine three steps: variation, evaluation, and selective retention ... it's more than supervised learning, which doesn't have the evaluation or retention.

A model trained by supervised learning has the variation and neither of the others:

> The missing part is the evaluation step. The generator of generative AI was pre-trained by supervised learning, and that leaves it no way at runtime to evaluate what it generates ... The novelty flickers into existence, but if its value is unrecognized, then it flickers away and is lost.

The exception he grants is augmentation: a language model given a way to evaluate and retain, with verifiers or formal validators, can discover, so long as the evaluation comes from a clear objective it can check.

[The hypothesis graph](/the-hypothesis-graph-semantic-memory-methodeutics) is that augmentation.

| Sutton's three steps | Generative AI | The hypothesis graph |
|---|---|---|
| Variation | ✓ | abduction: candidate causes, partly blind |
| Evaluation, from a clear goal | ✗ | a deterministic gate: the test passes or it does not |
| Selective retention | ✗ | the witnessed node, kept in a persistent store |

The retained value has to be *checkable*, not merely kept, which is the argument of [Truth Is Buildable](/truth-is-buildable): a claim is true only while its build passes. One worked run is in [the paper](/the-hypothesis-graph-semantic-memory-methodeutics), a post-cutoff compiler bug its maintainers were stuck on, where the inquiry built a fix that did not exist until it built it, every step replayable by a stranger. The same loop runs on real filed issues in [Speedrunning Open Source](/speedrunning-open-source). Sutton named the recipe in mid-May. The work was already running.
