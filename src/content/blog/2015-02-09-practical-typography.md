Typography is its own perceptual physics. Readability research predates computers by decades. Butterick's guide is the most opinionated, practical distillation of it. One person's durable principles, not a committee document.

### The pages that matter

- [Typography in ten minutes](https://practicaltypography.com/typography-in-ten-minutes.html) — the minimum viable knowledge
- [Summary of key rules](https://practicaltypography.com/summary-of-key-rules.html) — the full checklist
- [Line length](https://practicaltypography.com/line-length.html) — 45-90 characters per line
- [Line spacing](https://practicaltypography.com/line-spacing.html) — 120-145% of font size
- [Font size](https://practicaltypography.com/font-size.html) — never below 15-16px on screen
- [Page layout](https://practicaltypography.com/page-layout.html) — margins, whitespace, overall composition
- [Headings](https://practicaltypography.com/headings.html) — typographic hierarchy through size and weight
- [Maxims of page layout](https://practicaltypography.com/maxims-of-page-layout.html) — the principles behind the rules

### Key ingredients for an agent

Line length, line spacing, font size, paragraph spacing, and heading hierarchy are mechanical — the pages above give specific numbers. Read the relevant page and apply.

Font choice is not mechanical. It's the difference between a correct page and a page with character. System fonts are safe. A well-chosen web font is a voice. This is the one decision the agent should not make alone — ask the user what tone the page should carry, then propose options. Butterick's [font recommendations](https://practicaltypography.com/font-recommendations.html) are a good starting point for the conversation.
