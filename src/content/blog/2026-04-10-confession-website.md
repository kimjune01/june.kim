[ephemeral.website](/ephemeral-website) proved the pattern, one listen, then gone, but it was a dead drop. I could leave a message and only wonder whether it landed.

confession.website closes the loop. I record a voice note and get a link. The recipient listens once, and the audio burns on play. But now the burned link becomes a reply channel where they can record back to the same URL. One reply, one listen, then the whole exchange disappears.

Neither side has the other's name, number, or handle, so the shared URL is the whole relationship. When the TTL expires, the URL 404s and the server retains nothing — a subpoena requires something in storage, and if you apply that standard to every email server the distinction is clear.
