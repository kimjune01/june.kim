## The meta-finding (all three agents, codex-confirmed)

Word-level tells are fading; the durable 2026 signal is **structure + co-occurrence + thin reasoning**.
Frontier prose reads clean sentence by sentence, so detection moved up to document shape, formatting
reflexes, and density. Single instances are not proof; clustering is. This became the new **"Signal
weighting"** note at the top of the Patterns section (hard / high / medium / low tiers).

## Convergent signals (surfaced by ≥2 agents independently)

- **Negative parallelism** ("it's not X, it's Y") — the most-discussed AI tell of 2026 (corporate-doc
  incidence ~4x, 2023-2025). Folded as new variants on the existing entry (countdown "Not X. Not Y.",
  "the question isn't X, it's Y", correlatives).
- **Bold-lead bullet stack** ("**Header:** sentence" ×3+) — the defining visual fingerprint. New.
- **Participial summary tail** (", making it...", ", ensuring...") — the marquee grammatical tell;
  2-5x human rate, survives paraphrase. Expanded the old "-ing clauses" entry + grep signature.

## Folded into /humanize

## Tightening (same pass)

Consolidated five scattered "announce instead of state" entries — throat-clearing, deferred conclusion,
suspense labels, question framing, canned openers — into ONE **"Staged framing"** entry with seven
subtypes (those five + fragment hook + aphoristic closer) under a single shared rule and fix. Net: the
Patterns section gained ~6 tics' worth of coverage while dropping from a sprawl of overlapping prose
blocks to a tighter taxonomy. Top-level entry count held roughly flat; each entry now carries more and
many have grep signatures.

## Pruned (codex)
