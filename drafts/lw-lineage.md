## The epistemics canon (what the *content* bridges to — Verifiable Knowledge / hypothesis graph)

These ground the deeper claim, that a node carries its kill condition and a verdict comes from replay,
in LW's own vocabulary. Your `e10` sample already reached for the first one, which is the tell that it's
the right anchor.

- **Making Beliefs Pay Rent (in Anticipated Experiences)** — Yudkowsky. The kill-condition anchor: a
  belief that anticipates no experience can't be falsified. This *is* the hypothesis graph's edge in LW
  terms; cite it as the native statement of "every node names what would refute it."
  https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/a7n8GdKiAZRX86T5A/making-beliefs-pay-rent-in-anticipated-experiences
- **Local Validity as a Key to Sanity and Civilization** — Yudkowsky. The verification-step anchor for
  *Verifiable Knowledge*: trust rests on steps anyone can check locally, not on the authority of who said
  it. Maps onto "the verdict is the check's, not the checker's."
  https://www.lesswrong.com/posts/WQFioaudEH8R7fyhm/local-validity-as-a-key-to-sanity-and-civilization

## How to use this

- The **debut post** (the voice-gate research, written straight) opens against the institutional set:
  "LW now auto-rejects suspected LLM text. I red-teamed whether that gate can be fooled. Here's what it
  actually reads." Tier-1 posts are the framing; Tier-2 are the one-line epistemic grounding.
- The **repo** can carry a short `RELATED-WORK.md` with the same list, which also strengthens the DOI deposit.
- Keep it load-bearing: cite a post only where your argument actually leans on it. The research's own
  finding is that decorative citations get flagged as name-dropping. Don't pad the reference list to look native.
