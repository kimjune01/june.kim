# Outreach plan — artifact-first, referral-routed, synchronous

Rebuilt around what the evidence says works in 2026 (see the noise/doom-loop research). The cold
application is demoted to the *close*: the thing you send once a human already has a reason to read you,
not the thing that creates the reason. The earlier `funding-strategy.md` target table still holds for
*which* funders; this file replaces the *order of operations*.

## Why the inversion

Cold applications into grant/job pipelines are now a degraded channel: both sides run AI, volume is up
37–57%, funders are rationing (NIH caps PIs at 6/year), and a human may never read your message. The
system's own response is to route around it through trust networks (referral interviews are 35% more
likely to convert; some employers abandoned public postings) and un-fakeable signal (portfolio that
proves over résumé that claims; "authenticity" interviews). You are built for the channel that survived.
So we stop pushing on the broken one.

## The funnel (lead at the top, not the bottom)

### Layer 1 — Be seen: distribute the artifact where the right humans gather

Make the work arrive before you do, in the places where funders and labs read human-to-human and signal
still propagates. Goal: when an application later lands, the name is already known.

- **Alignment Forum / LessWrong.** This is nearly a direct line: LTFF and Open Phil grantmakers *are*
  this community. Post a distilled *Verifiable Knowledge* / hypothesis-graph piece framed to the
  oversight crowd (external replay defeats self-grading / sandbagging). Engagement here is read by the
  exact people who fund.
- **X / research Twitter.** The agent/evals/safety researchers. Two timestamped, un-fakeable hooks: the
  Verus capability-lift result, and the determinacy audit ("I audited all 728 SWE-bench Pro tasks").
- **Hacker News.** *How Not to Run SWE-bench Pro* is a natural HN piece (methodology honesty, walked-back
  number). Drives the coding-agent and eval crowd.
- **Manifund (public proposal).** Doubles as distribution and puts you in front of regrantors whose job
  is finding under-the-radar people.

### Layer 2 — The one human: substantive contact to the people you build on

The least-noisy cold email in existence is "I tested / extended / audited your thing, here is a repo you
can run." You have real, specific reasons to be in these inboxes that a sprayer does not. These people
are also connectors into funding and jobs. (Template: `email-artifact-first.md`.)

- **Marchal et al. (DeepMind), the "verification crisis" authors.** They named the problem; you built and
  demonstrated a mechanism for it. Highest-fit substantive contact.
- **The SWE-bench Pro team (Scale, Deng et al.).** You audited all 728 of their tasks for determinacy.
  That is a real finding they have reason to care about, independent of any ask.
- **Cooperative AI Foundation researchers.** "Agents who don't trust each other" is their mandate; reach a
  person, not just the form.
- **CoALA authors (Sumers et al.) / trajectory-assessment (Yehudai et al.).** Your semantic-memory and
  trajectory-level work extends theirs directly.
- **Traxia (arXiv 2606.08256).** Concurrent work on agent-native publishing; a peer/ally, not a funder.
  Convergence is worth a note.
- **Eval orgs: METR, Apollo, Redwood.** Reach a named researcher with the audit + the harness, not the
  careers portal.

[Fill: anyone you already know, or are one intro away from, in any of these. SFU contacts count. A 2nd-
degree intro beats every cold path above.]

### Layer 3 — Synchronous: be un-fakeable in real time

A live conversation is the one surface AI noise can't occupy, and it's where authenticity now gets judged.
One of these outweighs fifty emails this year.

- **A live `abductor` demo.** The strongest possible artifact: a stranger watches the gate regrade in
  minutes. Record it once (a 5-minute screencast is async-replayable proof), and offer it live.
- **A talk / reading-group slot.** AI-safety reading groups, an agents workshop, a lab's open seminar.
- **A podcast / Spaces.** The agent-reliability and epistemics angle is interview-ready.
- **A fellowship / program: MATS, Astra, Constellation.** Structured, mentor-backed, stipended. For
  breaking in independently this *is* the trust-network entry the research says to use. It converts cold
  into warm by construction and is a strong fit for your bridge-to-runway plan.

### Layer 4 — The close: applications (demoted, not dropped)

Now send the forms, with a human already primed. LTFF first (fast), Manifund in parallel (public),
Cooperative AI second (high-fit, slower). Drafts already written: `ltff-application.md`,
`manifund-proposal.md`, `email-cooperative-ai.md`, etc. They are the close, not the open.

## The "early and verifiably right" asset

The one credential noise can't manufacture: a timestamp can't be backdated. Deploy it explicitly. Lead
with "I called X on [date], archived, here's the proof" wherever you can: the determinacy / translation-
vs-discovery thesis, the dated provenance posts the paper already lists. This is unforgeable in a way no
pitch is, and it's most of why being read-early on the Forum/Twitter compounds.

## First two weeks (concrete)

1. Ship the distilled Forum/LessWrong post and one X thread (Layer 1). Record the 5-min abductor demo.
2. Send 3–5 Layer-2 substantive notes (DeepMind verification-crisis authors, SWE-bench Pro team,
   Cooperative AI). Artifact-first, no ask in the first touch beyond "curious what you think."
3. Apply to the next MATS/Astra/Constellation cohort if open; line up one talk or podcast.
4. Put LTFF + Manifund in (Layer 4) once 1–3 have started, so the name precedes the form.

The order matters more than the volume. Three warm, artifact-led touches beat fifty cold sends, and the
research is unambiguous on that now.
