# Probe target: the "epistemics is untrue by its own standard" dunk

Context: this is for a sober, citable epistemics paper, *What Cannot Be False Cannot Be True*. Its thesis: a claim earns nothing until it is exposed to a world that could refute it (verify regime), versus claims merely trusted/believed/taken-whole. The third state, **untrue**, is "no passing build" — including the unfalsifiable (string theory: cannot be false, therefore cannot be true). The paper grounds itself in real engineering failures with primary-source RCAs (Challenger/Rogers, Hyatt/NBS, Chernobyl/INSAG-7).

We want to add a counterclaim turning the paper's own standard on academic epistemology itself: an epistemology that answers only to other epistemology never faces the test it demands of everything else, so by its own trichotomy most of the field is **untrue** (edgeless, like string theory). The author calls this "epistemics incest."

The irony trap we must avoid: asserting this as an unverified bibliometric wager would itself be an unbuilt claim — the exact move the paper condemns. So the dunk must rest on receipts.

## Candidate passage (the dunk)

> The discipline that studies knowledge mostly declines to take its own test. Citation studies of analytic philosophy find that its references position the author among other philosophers rather than extend a thread of result — the report verbs evaluative ("overlooks," "exaggerates") where the sciences write "measures" and "shows" (Hyland 1999) — and its citation sentiment the most negative of the humanities, dissociation rather than build-on (Sula and Miller 2014). A century of this accumulates papers without the rising consensus that marks a field answerable to something outside it (Petrovich 2018). By the standard argued here, an epistemology that faces only other epistemology has built nothing the world could refute, and earns the verdict it hands to everything else.

## Evidence currently in hand

1. **Petrovich, E. (2018). "Accumulation of Knowledge in Para-Scientific Areas: The Case of Analytic Philosophy."** (arXiv:1802.05941; published in *Scientometrics*). Citation-context analysis of 60 papers from 5 top AP journals (Phil Review, Noûs, J Phil, Mind, PPR), 1950-2009. Analytic philosophy "mimics" the scientific ethos/self-image; it began accumulating after WWII but the accumulation is NOT matched by rising consensus — fragmentation into sub-fields. I.e., accumulates text without convergence.
2. **Hyland (1999)** (reported in Petrovich): in philosophy, citations are not used primarily to extend the thread of knowledge but to position the writer relative to views supported/opposed; evaluative report verbs ("overlook," "exaggerate") dominate vs neutral "present/report" in sciences.
3. **Sula & Miller (2014)** (reported in Petrovich): sentiment analysis across humanities journals incl. *J Phil* — philosophy showed the MOST NEGATIVE polarity; philosophers frequently dissociate from cited work.
4. **Cullars (1998)** (reported in Petrovich): philosophy citations point to books, "typically humanistic," not a cumulative research front.
