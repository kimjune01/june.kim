## Scope on the hypothesis-graph asset

**The lift is a bonus. Do not lead with it.** The paper is problem-driven, and its introduction names the four it solves: reasoning cost, agent trust, lost reasoning, and the review bottleneck. Those four are what it is for, and they are the Tier 2 rows above. The graph is the accountability substrate answering them, argued by construction rather than measured, because every node replays.

The capability lift is a separable second contribution that happens to be the part carrying an ablation. Having the only number makes it the tempting thing to quote, and quoting it first inverts the paper: it turns a memory-and-accountability result into a benchmark-style capability claim, which is both weaker ground and a fight with people who work on scaffolds. Reach for the review-bottleneck row first. Mention the lift second, as a bonus, or not at all.

That framing also places it on the ladder below: the hypothesis graph is the symmetric standard applied to agent memory, which is why the paper says it gives the agent a trail of verifiable knowledge. Same move as `/science-on-trial` and `/papiermark-credentials`, one rung down.

The lift claim is narrow on purpose, and quoting it wider than its scope is how it gets destroyed. It applies to bugs where **a green suite cannot discriminate**: the project's own tests pass for both the wrong narrow fix and the correct general one, so passing certifies nothing about the distinction that matters. Verus #2219 was chosen because it has that property, plus a post-cutoff date making it contamination-free. On a bug whose tests already separate right from wrong, the mechanism has nothing to add and the claim does not reach.

Carry the live limits with it. The lift is coverage-bound, so where the gate's grammar is silent the fix lands wide-but-broken rather than correct. One of four models cleared the bug arm and still could not implement the divergence case, an implementation wall the verdicts do not remove. The cross-model convergence shows reproducibility across workflows, not three models independently rediscovering the predicate. And the paper is under TMLR review rather than accepted, so it is a preprint that happens to carry an ablation.

Do not use it against a researcher working on agent harnesses or scaffolds. Against the ambient "just wait for a better model" or "just build a better scaffold" it is decisive, because those are exactly the two controls the ablation ran.

## Supporting, not settling: the reading collections

`/reading/scientific-method/` holds worked pages on the primary sources, one per thinker: `peirce-1878` (which named abduction and founded pragmatism), `popper-1934`, `mayo-2018`, `chamberlin-1890`, `platt-1964`, `fisher-1935`, `meehl-1967`, `kuhn-1962`, `hume-1739`, `mill-1843`, `bacon-1620`, `pearl-2000`, `ioannidis-2005`, `ramdas-2023`, `shafer-2021`, `feynman-1974`. `/reading/methodeutics/` is the textbook built on top, from the three modes through bi- and tri-abduction to the economy of research.

These settle nothing on their own, so they are never the asset. A bibliography does not answer a claim, and linking one at a stranger is the flag-planting the gates exist to stop.

They do exactly one job, and it is defensive. The most likely attack on the hypothesis-graph rows is that the Peircean framing is decoration, philosophy jargon draped over ordinary prompt engineering. ## The symmetric standard

June's stance is that the verification standard applies identically to humans and to agents. Entitlement to believe comes from a re-runnable check, never from who or what is making the claim. A senior engineer's assertion, a peer-reviewed paper, a maintainer's approval, and a model's output are all attestations, each worth exactly the check behind it.

Four assets are the same move at four scales, which is why they interchange: `/science-on-trial` at institutional scale (publication and peer review as attestation), `/papiermark-credentials` at individual scale (the degree and the CV), `/trust-is-a-cache` and `/verifiable-knowledge` as the general principle, and the benchmark audits at machine scale (a score as attestation). Pick the rung matching the thread's subject, not the rung you find most interesting.

This matters for targeting, because the AI half is consensus on HN and the human half is not. Nobody needs to be told to check model output. What people will not say out loud is that a human gatekeeper's role does not confer warrant either. So the trigger is a commenter who applies a strict verification standard to AI while granting humans entitlement by role, seniority, credential, or title.

Then apply the distinction that killed the 2026-07-21 candidates. Invoking a role is usually a *division-of-labor* claim (distinct checkers cover distinct blind spots, seniors supervise juniors) and the symmetric standard is fully compatible with that, so there is nothing to answer. It is a target only when the role is offered as the *warrant itself*: the claim is credible because a person with the title signed off, with no check named behind the signature. Read the whole comment before deciding which one it is, since the two look identical in a one-line quote.
